My father had been a somewhat well-known magician in his line of work — a quirky man who loved to travel.
He’d wander overseas without warning, leaving his kid behind, and try to make it up to me with strange souvenirs that made no sense.

His catchphrase on stage was, “I am a real magician!”
Apparently, part of his act involved handing a knife to a planted audience member and letting them pretend to stab him.
But one day, an overly excited spectator pulled out their own knife and slashed him for real.
He never regained consciousness — gone just like that.
