Age Eleven — Part 1

Two more years passed. I was now eleven, and Giselle was nine.

I had once sworn—delirious with fever from that awful cold—that I would give up magic for good. But, as the saying goes, once the fever fades, the vow cools with it. In truth, that resolve lasted maybe a day. Now, as always, I was back to training in magic.

At this point, I had already surpassed the chief in certain areas. I could see the limits of what could be achieved within this settlement.

I knew it now more than ever: I needed to see the outside world. If I stayed here, I would stagnate.

“Abel! We’re going hunting! Come out, you shut-in!”

Father’s voice pounded against my door. But it was no use. A sturdy barrier sealed my room; no one short of the chief could undo it from the outside.

I felt bad for him, but I had decided to lock myself away for several days to finish my research. There was a spell—one I had dreamed of using ever since I learned that magic existed in this world. It was nearly eighty percent complete now. I was determined to finish it in one go.

“Apologies, Father, but I’m busy. If it’s for hunting, I can lend you an Otem.”

Yes—after much trial and error, I had finally developed a magic circle that let Otems hunt. It contained multiple complex reactive behaviors and could respond to simple commands like “follow” or “wait.” Since it merely reacted to voice cues, I could easily add more commands through the magic circle. By designing the arms to move at the joints, the Otem could even fire a bow. It was efficient, too—capable of running for about five hours on a single charge of mana. Accurate enough with a bow, obedient, and far more useful than a single child.

When I showed it to the chief, he’d shouted, “A prodigy! Abel truly is a prodigy!” He’d even begun using the magic circle himself. Though it was too intricate for anyone else to manage, given its finicky mana balance.

“Abel! That’s not the point, and you know it! How many times must I say it?”

“I’m in the middle of important magical research! Please don’t disturb my concentration! If you keep banging on the door, I’ll be forced to activate soundproofing magic!”

“Giselle misses you too!”

“That’s a lie! I already told her I’d be isolating myself for three days! Giselle’s a patient girl!”

I took out a small wooden wand. A wand increases both precision and power in spellcasting.

This one was my own creation—infused with mana and engraved with a casting circuit that allows me to project magic circles into the air without chanting. It cuts out an entire step.

When I’d first made it, I was quite proud—but the chief had said, “Wands with projection circuits are common enough. Still, making one yourself is impressive.” It stung a bit, but he was right. Embedding a transfer circuit—the foundation of most magic—was only natural. Still, the conversation had strengthened my desire to one day see the world beyond this village.

Now, to add soundproofing to the barrier and completely shut myself off.

I gave the wand a flick, and a magic circle appeared in the air.

“নীরবতা沈黙を　দি……与……”

“Giselle really was crying! She said it hurt not to see you for three whole days!”

…That hit harder than I expected. I’d even given her an Otem modeled after my face to keep her company, but it clearly hadn’t worked. Maybe I should go check on her.

“But if I leave this room, you’ll just drag me along on the hunt, won’t you?”

“Of course.”

“Then that defeats the purpose! Don’t you think it’s cruel to Giselle?”

“You can play with her after we get back.”

“Hunting takes nearly half a day! You’re saying you want me to come out for her, yet you’ll take me away from her immediately? Isn’t that contradictory?”

“The fact that you’re planning to lock yourself away for three days is what’s strange here.”

“Fair point.”

I conceded—but I didn’t agree.

“Anyway, how are you using the toilet?”

“I’d rather not answer that; you’ll try to counter it. Don’t worry—it’s handled.”

Teleportation magic lets one instantly travel to a location marked by a prepared circle. I’d set up such circles in both my room and the toilet, each engraved on an Otem. Teleportation was an advanced spell, one my father didn’t know I could use. Since I’d carved the circle onto an existing toilet Otem, he’d never suspect it. My secret was safe.

Well—mostly. Since teleportation circles need periodic mana infusion to stay active, I had to recharge them every five hours. Teleportation consumes a lot of mana, but at this short range, that schedule sufficed.

It was a perfect plan… except I’d underestimated Giselle’s sisterly devotion. I couldn’t leave her crying like that.

“I’ll come out once you leave for the hunt.”

“Then I’ll wait. No hunting until you come out.”

“I’m fine with that—but Mother will be angry.”

“Then come out before she gets angry.”

There was no end to this back-and-forth. I raised my wand again and traced a circle in the air.

“All right, I’ll come out once I finish this. Just wait a bit.”

“পুতুল人形よ　দখল踊れ”

I cast the spell on an Otem sitting on my desk, then carried it to the door.

“Did you just chant something?” Father asked.

“少シ待っデくだサい,” the Otem replied in a jerky imitation of my voice. Its mouth moved stiffly as it spoke.

It was a talking Otem—something I’d built based on a theory I’d read in the chief’s library and modified myself. The voice was a little rough, but that was fine for a quick test. Perfecting tone and variation would take more tuning. Still, it should be enough to keep Father distracted at the door.

“Father, where is Giselle right now?”

“Giselle? Out in the yard, I believe.”

Mother had gone to the square for shopping, so the coast was clear—and the toilet unoccupied.

I moved to the corner of my room, raised my wand, and quietly chanted with the volume lowered.

“আম我を　বহন運べ”

Light streamed from the wand’s tip, wrapping around my body. I shut my eyes against the glare—and when I opened them, I was in the toilet.

Perfect.

Now, to check on Giselle. Maybe I could even bring her into my room to keep me company. I hadn’t included her in the isolation plan because I thought Father would panic, but if she was crying, then this was the right call from the start.

“Abel! Are you still not coming out!?” Father’s voice echoed from upstairs.

“少シ待っデくだサい.”

“You’ve been saying that for ages!”

“少シ待っデくだサい.”

…Yeah, I’d better hurry. That trick wouldn’t hold up for long.
